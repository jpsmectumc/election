Official Guidelines for the Conduct of Campaigning

JPSME COMMISSION ON ELECTIONS (COMELEC)

In strict accordance with the mandate provided under Article VI of The JPSME Electoral Code, the Commission on Elections hereby promulgates these comprehensive guidelines to govern the authorization, ethical standards, and platform dissemination for all cleared candidates and political parties.

I. Authorization and Campaign Duration

Pursuant to Section 22, the execution of any promotional or partisan activities is strictly bound by temporal and legal boundaries established by the Commission:

  • Official Campaign Window: The specific window for campaigning shall be determined solely by the Commission. Any campaigning conducted outside of this authorized period shall be considered a grave violation and grounds for immediate disqualification.
  • Candidacy Clearance Prerequisite: No member or partisan group shall initiate campaign operations until their Certificate of Candidacy (COC) has been officially screened, verified, and included in the published Official List of Qualified Candidates.
  • Finality of Close: All campaign actions must cease precisely at the time dictated by the Official Election Calendar, ensuring the transition into the period of election silence.

II. Platform Equity and Media Standards

To preserve democratic integrity, the Commission enforces strict parity across all promotional formats:

  • Digital Media Parity: The Multimedia Team shall be responsible for ensuring that all candidates are provided with equal exposure on official JPSME – CTUMC digital platforms. The Commission guarantees that no candidate receives preferential treatment regarding graphic layouts, posting frequency, or overall visibility.
  • Neutrality of Institutional Facilities: No candidate shall be permitted to utilize official organization resources, funds, or equipment for personal campaign gains or advantages. All JPSME – CTUMC assets must remain completely non-partisan.
  • Vetting of Materials: All digital graphics, statements, and campaign literature must be submitted to the Multimedia and Secretariat units for review before public dissemination to ensure compliance with institutional decorum.

III. Ethical Conduct and Prohibited Practices

Candidates and their campaigns must reflect the highest professional and academic standards of the Department:

  • Standard of Fair Play: All candidates and their supporters are mandated to conduct themselves with the utmost dignity, professional courtesy, and respect toward their fellow students and the Commission.
  • Campaign Ethics: The dissemination of libelous statements, the engagement in personal attacks, and the intentional destruction of an opponent's campaign materials are strictly prohibited and shall be met with severe disciplinary action.
  • Vote Buying: The act of offering any form of incentive, monetary or otherwise, in exchange for a vote is classified as a grave electoral offense and is strictly forbidden.
  • Coercion: Intimidating or threatening any voter or member of the Commission to influence the outcome of the election is strictly prohibited and constitutes a grave offense.
