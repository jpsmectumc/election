Official Guidelines for the Conduct of Elections

JPSME COMMISSION ON ELECTIONS (COMELEC)

In strict accordance with the administrative powers and electoral mandates provided under Articles I, III, VII, and VIII of The JPSME Electoral Code, the Commission on Elections hereby promulgates these comprehensive guidelines to govern voter eligibility, precinct security, polling operations, and digital ballot integrity.

I. Voter Franchise and Eligibility Verification

Pursuant to Section 14, the right to exercise the electoral franchise is strictly limited to members who meet the following organizational criteria:

  • Academic Enrollment: The voter must be an officially enrolled student within the Mechanical Engineering program.
  • Good Organizational Standing: The student must be a registered member of the JPSME in good standing.
  • Identity Verification: Voters must present their valid institutional Identification Card (ID) or complete the designated electronic verification protocols established by the Commission prior to accessing the ballot.

II. Digital Polling Operations and System Integrity

To ensure administrative efficiency and prevent external manipulation, the casting of ballots shall follow rigorous structural controls:

  • Technical Enforcement: Under Section 25, the Technical Team is strictly responsible for maintaining the absolute integrity and security of the electronic or physical ballot.
  • Robust Authentication: In digital elections, the electronic voting system must employ robust authentication measures to prevent multiple voting or unauthorized system access.
  • Calendar Contingencies: Per Section 11, in the event of emergencies such as class suspensions, technical failures, or force majeure, the Chairman—or the Commission by a majority vote—reserves the exclusive right to adjust the voting hours or dates to prevent member disenfranchisement.

III. Precinct Security and Election Silence

To safeguard the solemnity and independence of the democratic process, strict behavioral boundaries are enforced across all physical and digital spaces:

  • Absolute Election Silence: On the designated election day, no person shall be allowed to engage in any form of campaigning within the designated physical voting area or through any official digital communication channels of the organization.
  • Polling Vicinity Restraints: Pursuant to Section 23, no campaign activities of any kind shall be allowed on the day of the election or within the immediate vicinity of the polling areas.
  • Ballot Confidentiality: Polling setups or digital interfaces must adhere to the principle of secret balloting. Visual or electronic monitoring of a voter while casting their vote is strictly prohibited.

IV. Electoral Fraud and Severe Offenses

The Commission maintains zero tolerance for activities that compromise the credibility of the election returns:

  • System Manipulation: Any attempt to manipulate the voting system, engage in double-voting, or coerce members into voting for a specific candidate shall result in immediate candidate disqualification and a formal report to the Student Affairs office.
  • Digital Sabotage: Any attempt to hack, disrupt, or compromise the integrity of the digital voting platforms or Commission databases is classified as a grave offense.
  • Coercion and Intimidation: Intimidating or threatening any voter or member of the Commission to influence the outcome of the election is strictly forbidden.
  • Disqualification Sanctions: Under Section 32, the Commission, by a majority vote of its members, may disqualify any candidate found guilty of an electoral offense and bar them from running in any future JPSME elections.
