Official Guidelines for Counting and Canvassing

JPSME COMMISSION ON ELECTIONS (COMELEC)

In strict accordance with the administrative powers and electoral mandates provided under Articles I, II, VII, and IX of The JPSME Electoral Code, the Commission on Elections hereby promulgates these comprehensive guidelines to govern ballot verification, vote tabulation transparency, and the final proclamation of winners.

I. Transparency and Candidate Watchers

To ensure absolute credibility and institutional integrity, the processing of election returns shall be subjected to open oversight:

  • Appointment of Watchers: Pursuant to Section 26, every candidate shall be entitled to designate one (1) official watcher.
  • Observation Rights: Authorized watchers shall be permitted to closely observe the tabulation and verification of results conducted by the Technical Team to ensure total transparency.
  • Non-Interference Rule: Watchers are strictly confined to the role of observers; they are prohibited from touching any system data, physical logs, or interfering with the operational functions of the Commission members.

II. Board of Canvassers and System Tabulation

The consolidation of election data shall strictly follow the structural hierarchy established under the electoral framework:

  • Composition of the Board: Pursuant to Section 27, the Chairman, the Secretary, and the Technical Team shall collectively serve as the Board of Canvassers.
  • Mandatory Tally Duties: The Board of Canvassers is explicitly tasked with tallying the votes and certifying the accuracy of the final results before any public announcement.
  • Digital Authentication Check: In accordance with the Technical Team's duties, the system logs and digital authentication tokens must be audited to verify that no duplicate voting or unauthorized data injection occurred prior to the extraction of the final tally.

III. Determination and Verification of Results

The finality and binding nature of the election outcomes are subject to precise certification and tie-breaking protocols:

  • Finality of Official Tally: Under Section 34, the results generated by the Technical Team and verified by the Board of Canvassers are considered final and binding once the Chairman formally signs the Certificate of Canvass.
  • Tie-Breaking Resolution: Pursuant to Section 36, in the event of an exact tie between two or more candidates, the Commission shall resolve the deadlock via a public "Toss of a Coin" or "Draw of Lots" to ensure a fair and transparent resolution.

IV. Post-Election Protests and Timelines

Voters and candidates are provided a strict, legally mandated window to contest certified tallies:

  • Right to Protest: Any member wishing to contest the results must file a formal, sworn petition with the Chairman.
  • Peremptory Deadline: Per Section 35, the petition must be filed within a strict period of twenty-four (24) hours following the official proclamation of the winners. Failure to file within this window renders the results incontestable.
