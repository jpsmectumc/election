Operational Directives

Official schedules, procedural rules, and regulations governing the active phases of the current election cycle.

I. Guidelines for Filing of Candidacy

In strict accordance with the mandate provided under Article IV of The JPSME Electoral Code, the Commission has officially promulgated the comprehensive guidelines to govern the qualification, documentation, and submission process for all members seeking elective office.

Key Provisions Include:

  • Mandatory Qualifications: Candidates must possess bona fide student status within the Mechanical Engineering Department, active organizational membership in good standing, and an integrity of record free from major disciplinary infractions.
  • Documentary Requirements: All candidates are strictly required to utilize the official Certificate of Candidacy (COC) template. No alternative formats will be accepted.
  • Protocol for Submission: Outlines the rigorous administrative chain of custody for direct and digital submissions to the Commissioners on Screening.

II. Guidelines for the Conduct of Campaigning

In strict accordance with the mandate provided under Article VI of The JPSME Electoral Code, the Commission on Elections has promulgated these comprehensive guidelines to govern the authorization, ethical standards, and platform dissemination for all cleared candidates and political parties.

Key Provisions Include:

  • Authorization & Duration: All promotional activities are strictly bound by the Official Campaign Window, requiring candidates to be officially cleared before initiating any campaign operations.
  • Platform Equity & Media Standards: Ensures equal exposure across official digital platforms and mandates the vetting of all campaign materials by the Commission prior to public dissemination.
  • Ethical Conduct & Prohibited Practices: Enforces the highest standard of fair play, explicitly prohibiting negative campaigning, vote buying, coercion, and libelous statements.

III. Guidelines for the Conduct of Elections

In strict accordance with the administrative powers and electoral mandates provided under Articles I, III, VII, and VIII of The JPSME Electoral Code, the Commission has promulgated these comprehensive guidelines to govern voter eligibility, precinct security, polling operations, and digital ballot integrity.

Key Provisions Include:

  • Voter Eligibility & Verification: The electoral franchise is strictly limited to officially enrolled Mechanical Engineering students who are registered JPSME members in good standing, subject to rigorous identity verification protocols.
  • System Integrity & Polling Operations: Mandates robust electronic authentication to prevent unauthorized system access and vests the Commission with the authority to adjust schedules in the event of technical failures or emergencies.
  • Election Silence & Security: Enforces absolute election silence on voting day, ensuring strict ballot confidentiality and prohibiting any campaign activities within polling vicinities or official digital channels.
  • Electoral Fraud Sanctions: Maintains zero tolerance for system manipulation, digital sabotage, coercion, and intimidation, with violations punishable by immediate disqualification and formal disciplinary reports.

IV. Guidelines for Counting and Canvassing

In strict accordance with the administrative powers and electoral mandates provided under Articles I, II, VII, and IX of The JPSME Electoral Code, the Commission has promulgated these comprehensive guidelines to govern ballot verification, vote tabulation transparency, and the final proclamation of winners.

Key Provisions Include:

  • Transparency & Observation: Guarantees the right of each candidate to appoint an official watcher to closely observe the tabulation and verification of results without operational interference.
  • System Tabulation & Auditing: Establishes the Board of Canvassers to tally votes and mandates rigorous digital authentication checks by the Technical Team to ensure absolute data integrity.
  • Proclamation & Tie-Breaking: Defines the finality of the officially certified tally and outlines transparent resolution protocols, such as a public draw of lots, for exact electoral ties.
  • Post-Election Protests: Provides a strict twenty-four (24) hour peremptory deadline following the official proclamation for the filing of any formal, sworn petitions contesting the results.
