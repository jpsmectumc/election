Campaign Guidelines & Operational Directives

JPSME COMMISSION ON ELECTIONS (COMELEC)

The Commission on Elections hereby promulgates these comprehensive operational directives to govern the five phases of the electoral process: filing, campaigning, dialogue, election, and canvassing. These guidelines enforce strict adherence to transparency, fairness, and neutrality.

I. General Operational Directives

  • Transparency and Neutrality: All electoral proceedings shall be conducted with absolute transparency and impartiality by the Commission.
  • Unopposed Candidates: Candidates running unopposed must undergo a Mandatory Vote of Confidence. To be proclaimed the winner, an unopposed candidate must obtain a simple majority (50% + 1) of the total votes cast for that specific position, measured against "Abstain" votes.
  • Presidential Contingency: If the sole candidate for the office of the President fails to meet the required electoral threshold, the Commission shall declare a Failure of Election for the Presidency and execute a Special Election as soon as possible.

II. Guidelines on Campaigning

The campaign period allows candidates to present their platforms to the electorate. Strict adherence to these rules is mandatory to maintain a clean and orderly election.

  • Authorized Campaign Period: All campaign activities must be strictly confined to the official Campaign Period as defined in the Election Calendar. Premature campaigning is prohibited.
  • Multimedia Clearance: All campaign materials, including digital publications, videos, and printed assets, must receive prior clearance from the Commission before distribution.
  • Prohibited Conduct: Negative campaigning, ad hominem attacks, smear campaigns, or any activities aimed at destroying the reputation of another candidate or the organization are strictly prohibited.
  • Enforcement: Violations of these campaign guidelines may result in sanctions, up to and including disqualification by the Commission.

III. Miting de Avance and Dialogue

  • Mandatory Attendance: All official candidates are required to attend the Miting de Avance to present their platforms and participate in dialogue.
  • Member Inquiry: The Miting de Avance shall provide a structured forum for the student body to respectfully direct inquiries to the candidates regarding their platforms.

IV. Election Day and Canvassing Protocol

  • Authenticated Balloting: The election shall utilize the official, authenticated digital balloting system to strictly enforce a one-voter-one-vote mandate.
  • Automated Tabulation: The canvassing of votes shall be conducted via an automated tabulation process to ensure speed, accuracy, and absolute integrity of the results.
  • Official Proclamation: The Commission shall formally proclaim the winners upon the certification of the final canvassed results.
