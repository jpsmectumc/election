Official Guidelines for the Filing of Candidacy

JPSME COMMISSION ON ELECTIONS (COMELEC)

In strict accordance with the mandate provided under Article IV of The JPSME Electoral Code, the Commission on Elections hereby promulgates these comprehensive guidelines to govern the qualification, documentation, and submission process for all members seeking elective office.

I. Mandatory Qualifications for Candidates

Pursuant to Section 14, a member's eligibility for candidacy is contingent upon the satisfaction of the following criteria at the time of filing:

  • Bona Fide Student Status: The prospective candidate must be an officially enrolled student within the Mechanical Engineering Department for the current academic year.
  • Organizational Membership: The individual must be a registered member of JPSME in good standing, demonstrating active participation or aspiring commitment to the organization's objectives.
  • Integrity of Record: The candidate must be free from any pending major disciplinary records or pending academic probation as verified by the Commission.

II. Documentary Requirements and Prescribed Format

To ensure administrative uniformity and legal clarity, the following documentary standards are mandated:

  • Use of Official Prescribed Format: All candidates are strictly required to utilize the official Certificate of Candidacy (COC) template provided by the Commission. No alternative formats, informal letters, or self-drafted documents shall be recognized as valid.
  • Contents of the COC: Per Section 15, the document must explicitly state the candidate's full legal name, residence, and the specific elective position sought.
  • Affirmation of Eligibility: The COC must include a formal statement affirming that the candidate meets all constitutional and organizational requirements for the office.
  • Sworn Undertaking: The filing must contain a signed undertaking to support and defend the JPSME Constitution and to faithfully fulfill the duties of the office upon election.
  • Statement of Truthfulness: The candidate shall certify under oath that all information provided is true and correct. Any material misrepresentation shall constitute grounds for disqualification.
  • Platforms: The candidate shall prepare a list of platforms, including a brief explanation of each.

III. Protocol for Submission and Verification

The transition of candidacy documents shall follow a rigorous administrative chain of custody:

  • Direct Submission to Commissioners: Completed COCs, including all necessary attachments, must be submitted directly to the Commissioners on Screening or the Secretariat within the timeframe established by the Official Election Calendar.
  • Digital Filing: Electronic submission through official channels designated by the Commission shall be deemed equivalent to physical filing, provided authenticity can be verified.
  • Acknowledgement of Receipt: Upon the physical or digital turnover of documents, the Commission shall issue a formal acknowledgement; this receipt serves as the candidate's only valid proof of timely filing.
  • Screening Authority: Under Section 17, the Commissioners on Screening are vested with the authority to motu proprio disqualify any submission that utilizes an unauthorized format, contains fraudulent data, or fails to meet established criteria.
  • Multiple Candidacies: No member shall file a Certificate of Candidacy for more than one elective position. Filing for multiple positions shall result in automatic disqualification unless voluntarily corrected within the prescribed period.

IV. Rules on Unopposed and Vacant Positions

  • Unopposed Candidates: In instances where only one valid COC is filed for a position, the candidate must undergo a Mandatory Vote of Confidence per Section 24. A simple majority (50% + 1) of "Yes" votes is required for proclamation.
  • Failure of Candidacy: If no valid COC is filed by the deadline, a "Failure of Candidacy" shall be declared, and the position shall subsequently be filled via the Appointive Power of the incoming President-elect, subject to Executive Board approval.

V. Withdrawal and Finality

  • Voluntary Withdrawal: Any candidate may formally withdraw their intent to run by submitting a written and signed statement of withdrawal to the Commissioners on Screening at any time prior to the commencement of voting.
  • Finality of Deadlines: Per Section 12, all deadlines for filing are peremptory; failure to comply with the set schedule shall result in the automatic forfeiture of the right to participate in the current election cycle.
  • Correction of Deficiencies: The Commission may allow candidates to correct clerical or non-substantive deficiencies within a period prescribed by the Commissioners on Screening, provided that such corrections do not alter eligibility requirements or filing deadlines.
  • Official List of Candidates: Upon completion of screening, the Commission shall publish the Official List of Qualified Candidates. Only individuals appearing on said list shall be recognized as official candidates for the election.
